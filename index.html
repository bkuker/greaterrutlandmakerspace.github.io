About

A group of Rutland area residents and local organizations, led by Rutland Economic Development Corporation (REDC) and Castleton University, are working to start a Makerspace in Rutland, VT.

What is a Makerspace?

A Makerspace is a community work environment with shared equipment, available to all for a monthly or yearly membership fee. The community would consist of for profit and non-profit companies, startup companies, local school representatives and students, DIY enthusiasts, and hobbyists.

Equipment might include:

  • 3d Printers
  • Laser Cutters
  • Woodworking
  • Metalworking
  • Welding
  • Electronics
  • Programming
  • CAD
  • Much More!

Classes in related fields and equipment use would be offered. Shared work benches would be included with the membership, but reserved desk and small office working spaces will be available for rent also.

Get Involved

The most important part of a Makerspace is you! For the Greater Rutland Makerspace to succeed we need a group of interested, excited members who want to learn new skills, mentor others, teach classes for both members and the public, and help make the dream a reality. Whether you are a hobbiest with bigger dreams than fit in your basement, want to join others in creating large projects, or need space and resouces to get your new business up and running, we want you to join in!

We have assembled a team of interested individuals and possible funding sources to get the makerspace going. We would now like your input regarding membership and equipment to try to better understand the needs, desires, and interest of local makers. We will need to grow our membership to a level where the Makerspace is an independent, successful organization.

Your response to the survey below, and the friends and fellow makers in the Rutland area that you can connect with us are key!
