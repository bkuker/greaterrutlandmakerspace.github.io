About

A group of Rutland area residents and local organizations, led by Rutland Economic Development Corporation (REDC) and Castleton University, are working to start a Makerspace in Rutland, VT.

What is a Makerspace?

A Makerspace is more than just a shared workshop with all the tools you don’t have space for or couldn’t afford on your own. A Makerspace is a community of people sharing their knowledge, experience, and ideas in addition to tools, equipment, and workshop space. Its members are a diverse group featuring hobbyists, DIY enthusiasts, local school representatives and students as well as start-up, non-profit, and for-profit companies.

Equipment might include:

  • 3D Printers
  • Laser Cutters
  • Woodworking
  • Metalworking
  • Welding
  • Electronics
  • Jewelry Making
  • Programming
  • CNC & Computer-Aided Design
  • Much More!

Access to the space and equipment is available to everyone for a monthly, or yearly, membership fee, with the option to reserve personal workbench or small office space for an additional fee.

Members of the community, as well as of the Makerspace, would also be offered opportunities to teach or enroll in classes in related fields and proper equipment use.

Get Involved

The most important part of a Makerspace is you! For the Greater Rutland Makerspace to succeed, we need a group of interested, excited members who want to learn new skills and share their skills through mentoring others or teaching classes. Whether you are a hobbyist with dreams that are too big for your basement, an innovator that just needs the space and resources to get your new business up and running, or an outgoing individual that loves working with on projects with others, we want you to join us!

So far we have assembled an initial team of interested individuals and a set of possible sources for funding in order to get the Makerspace going. What we need now is your input on membership and equipment in order to better understand the needs, desires, and interests of you, our local makers. Our goal is to grow our membership to a level where the Makerspace can be an independent, successful organization.
